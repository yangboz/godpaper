Interfacepublic interface IXray
Implementors AbstractChessGasket

The term X-ray attack was apparently originated by Kenneth Harkness. On page 25 of the April 1947 Chess Review, in his series Picture Guide to Chess, he mentioned forks and then wrote [1]: There is another type of double attack in which the targets are threatened in one direction. The attacking piece threatens two units, one behind the other, on the same rank, file or diagonal. This double threat has lacked a good descriptive name. We suggest ‘X-Ray’ attack.
